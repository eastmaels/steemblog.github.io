Spotting occultism in Batman v Superman and Knights of Badassdom.


Can we talk about some really weird and esoteric shit for a moment?  



So, above is a Screen Shot from Batman V Superman Dawn of Justice 2016 Extended Ultimate Edition.  Now, I don’t know about you, but that thing looks a lot like what I think satan is supposed to look like.  Giant horns.  Weird eyes.  Floating black cube thingies.  Christ like dude in the blood water for some ritualistic baptism.  That looks like some weird occult shit.  It’s totally freaky.  It’s also not even the bad guy.  It just makes a quick cameo in the movie.


So, I went down this rabbit hole.  Honestly, I’ve been down a lot of rabbit holes.  Some of my buddies think I’ve lost it a little bit, but hopefully some randos on the internet will hear me out.


I was listening to an interview on Fade to Black with Jimmy Black.  He was interviewing David Wilcock.  David talks about a lot of stuff, but in regard to this article he was talking about occult and Luciferian messaging in Batman V Superman.  David says puts some of the following statements together to 


“Jesse Eisenberg plays Lex Luther.  Lex Luther is how they (the occult) see Jesus Christ…  He’s the guy trying to detonate the illuminati.  (He cuts his hand, his blood goes in the Kryptonian cadaver to make a monster.) The monster has a glowing heart, which is the glowing heart of christ.  They see it as a demon.  (You later see his cut hand with his bloody palm, so they are not being subtle).  They have a picture of Lucifer in the movie as a fallen angle.



Eisenberg says himself during the movie while looking at that picture: “The devil’s don’t come from hell beneath us.  No, they come from the sky.”


He talks about Superman and Batman actually being aspects of Lucifer as in both represent Lucifer.  



In their version Lucifer defeats Christ and his glowing heart monster



and then sends Christ to prison.





Well, ok, this explanation makes some sense to me, but where else can I see it?  A buddy of mine who likes D&D said go watch Knights of Badassdom.  The movie is horrible all around, but it follows a similar pattern.  It’s an occult version of the killing of Jesus hidden in a terrible movie about Larping in the woods.




They summon a bad girl from a parallel universe that turns into a giant demon monster.





The monster is crucified.



Then he’s stabbed while crucified.



The hero sings a ritualistic death metal song.



The lyrics I can hear are “Cast down into thine dark crypt.  Shadows of our dead minions drag.  Thee into the abyss.  (His soul) Rises bringing his deadliest gifts.  His soul is devoured in wrath. The song continues


Lifeless husk.  From you raise you blade defeat.  The vile snake your heart.  His wings turn the sky to black as he makes this world thy tomb.


The minion attacks relentlessly.

and the minion vanishes.

The monster really dies completely, and the heroes say drop to the nether realms, bitch.


The girlfriend says “”You have questionable taste in women.”  which is a reference to the story where the monster came from a succubus that resembled his girlfriend, but also seems linked to an emasculation of Jesus.




So, that’s my dose of weird occult imagery in modern movies depicting Christ as the monster, Lucifer as the savior, and is clearly a weird distortion of the stories you may have read about in the Bible etc.
