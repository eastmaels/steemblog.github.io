Mining ZCash and converting to Steem through blocktrades and Nicehash


Thought I’d drop a little update on the folks who care about mining.




I started trying to use Nicehash for both mining and then sell hashing power service, but the miner crashed windows 10.  So, now I have a few different setups using claymore’s zec miner which is on version 9.1.




I have my main PC which is running windows 10 on a fast i7 and has a Radeon 480 in it.


I have my wife’s PC which is running windows 10 on a slow intel chip with limited ram.  It has a Radeon 470 in it.


I have a mining rig that has 4 Radeon 470s and 1 Radeon 460 in it.




Each PC pulls between 70-90 watts just for being on.


The Radeon 480 pulls about 150 watts when it’s mining.


The Radeon 470 pull about 120 watts when they are mining.


The Radeon 460 pulls about 70 or so watts when it is mining.




The Radeon 480 is making 220 H/s.  The Radeon 470 is making 200 H/S.  The Radeon 460 is making 96 H/S.



My total hashing power is about 1316/s.  The total watts that are related to mining are 1010 (and about another 200 for CPUs that I would have kept on anyway).



My electricity costs $0.2/KWh so I use about $4.60 of electricity for mining each day.


BTC to USD is $773 USD/ BTC


Mining through nice hash pay fluctuates between .006-.011 btc per day for 1k H/S.  I think I’m averaging about $7/day right now in revenue.  




So, overall it looks like I’m making $2.40/day doing this.  Given that it cost me about $1600 for everything it doesn’t look like I’m going to get paid back any time soon.  The other thing to consider is that as my wife noted electricity is paid with after tax money.  So, real electrical costs because of taxes are probably closer to $0.26/ KWH.  



If I can figure out a way to get electricity cheaply then this looks pretty worth it.  $7 a day for 365 days is more than $2g a year, but it does seem like trying to do this with expensive ass electricity in New England doesn’t quite work out.



All that said, my heat is electric too.  And all of this stuff gives off heat.  So, to some extent I can discount my electric heating bill by how much I’m producing by mining.  Here’s an example of what that looks like.



My ASIC miner which is an Antminer S7-LN uses 740 watts to make 2.7 TH/S.  My space heater for my attic where I work and blog from requires 640 watts.  The real space heater is a little better than my ASIC device, but they work roughly the same.  So, if I assume that the ASIC is only using 100 watts to make 2.7 TH/S and the rest is going to heat then that’s pretty good actually.  I turn it off at night.  I turn it on in the morning.  My space is warm and some bitcoin get made.



Time to invent a free energy device.
