Eff this place! Let's buy a cruise ship and start a new country.


If you haven’t heard of the concept of Seasteading you’re missing out.  It’s the idea that we can collectively start a country in the sea.  If you haven’t noticed all the land around here has been claimed by 197 governments.  They have not proven very friendly when people start declaring their sovereignty.  That means 197 governments monopolize the control of 7 billion people.  That ratio seems very off to me and seems to allow an unusually large amount of malfeasance. 



Anyway, a while back I remember reading about this country that this guy had formed in the sea.  It’s called Sealand.  He took over an abandoned platform created by the Brits in WWII and declared it a country.  He was dragged into court about it, and the court ruled they had no jurisdiction.  He created a country and it was de facto recognized by the most (possibly 2nd most) imperialistic nation on the planet.


So, then I got to thinking about the Seasteading Institute.  Their plan is to build a giant  floating city from scratch and dangle it in coastal waters of a friendly nation that’s willing to make them mostly autonomous sorta like a floating Hong Kong.  That’s all well and good, but everyone knows that it’s significantly cheaper to buy something used than to buy something brand new.  I also don’t want to rely on the kindness of people that tend to be murderous psychopaths (aka politicians).


So, I started looking at container ships.  They are big, but you’d have to retrofit the hell out of them.  Then I stumbled on this gem of an article that talks about buying cruise ships.  I stopped looking at the container ships that would need serious overhauling and started looking at some sweet used cruise ships.  I mean they are built for lots of people.  There are also floating hotels, but they don’t seem like they would do ok 12-200 miles off shore.








So, here’s what I’m currently thinking is best.



It looks like this!



So, the thing costs $4.5Million.  But it has 356 passenger cabins.  That’s $12362/cabin.  Some cabins are smaller and some are larger so the price will likely vary from a minimum of $10k-15k relative to the investment in the ship.  You can also track it by person, which is $900 or $5000/person.  The rooms are a little cramped, but the life can be pretty good cruise ship.


How would the thing work?  Well, you draft a proposed constitution and declaration of independence.  You crowd source or get bank financing.  You give citizenship to everyone that owns a room or bed.  You can issue your own currency that can be spent on the boat or traded.  You allow people more freedom than they’d experience anywhere else.  You could also issue money to pay people that work on the boat.  The people on the boat could rent out rooms, sell services, or sell goods in order to buy food and supplies not provided by the boat itself.  If you’re allowing more freedom you can do things like run a casino that people wouldn’t otherwise be allowed to do in their home country.  I would think tourism could be a big hit and since the thing is already setup as an entertainment vessel it’s likely it could be a go to destination whenever it docks.


You could slap some aquaponics in there to help grow food on the ship.  Before you know it you have a country, a currency, a good starting point, and many people can enjoy either a pleasant retirement, a good cruise, or figure out how to raise a family.  The boat I’m showing there has a family room and crew quarters that could be used for teachers etc.  Maybe we could start something on it.




Do you have $12k as a starting point for a room and citizenship in a floating country?  Would you trade currency issued to you, digital or print, by the government of your floating ship?  Wanna buy a country/cruise ship with me?
