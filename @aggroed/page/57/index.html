Steem Impact Forum- Venezuela - the recording

I think we had a pretty successful meeting today. I’m proud to have hosted a small digital forum with a few members of the Venezuelan community as well as @pgarcgo of the Cervantes Spanish community. The topics ranged a few places. There were some touching stories of poverty in the streets. I’m stuck with the image of a few children eating worms out of a can to find enough food. There are stories of financial hardship in that the minimum wage in the country isn’t enough to buy groceries. There are clearly some deep rooted economic conditions that are shaping the lives of the every day people.

I was a little surprised to hear about some in the community are aok living happy lives with more than enough to meet their needs. The narrative of haves and have nots ins’t really coming through the American media.

I’m wondering how do we help so we’re investigating using a team of Venezuelan programmers to put together a Steem centric job board. We were able to raise a few thousand Sp in delegations for Cervantes while on air. I passed Cervantes a witness vote to help them along and build up the Spanish speaking community on Steem.

Fiddling

During the broadcast I was fiddling with a bunch of twenty dollar bills I recently took out to pay our baby sitter over the next week or two. I was figiting with them thinking about the fact that one of those $20 bills is the same amount of money that an engineer makes in Venezuela in a whole month. That’s staggering to me.

That’s part of why I want to see the walls of commerce come down. It’s somewhat exploitive to start to use Venezuelans to build a platform for job seeking, but once it’s up we should see a better system to connect job seekers with employers or more like gig posters with gig fulfillers. I imagine as the barrier drops we’ll see more and more people getting hired and as the supply of labor diminishes we should see prices (wages) rise.

It’s also staggering to think that a single upvote of mine of which I can drop ten per day is the equivalent ot a monthly salary for many Venezuelans. It puts that into a better perspective… that said I have 16k active SP and my voting strength is 70% that means if you own 11k Steem and solely vote for Venezuelans and similarly economically disadvantaged people that you could support 300 people a month with a vote that covers the same as their wages for a month.

Muchas Gracias

Anyway, great thanks to those that were on the panel, joined us live during the show, and shared their experiences and their thoughts with us.

Steem Impact Forum: Venezuela with @pgarcgo, @amanardis, @drmaizo, @feuerbolt, @spicyshay, @kravelious, @michelcamacaro

Starting in just under 3hrs the Peace, Abundance, and Liberty Network is pleased to host the Steem Impact Forum focusing on Venezuela. Venezuela is having a horrific experience of watching their currency hyperinflate. A small salvation to the economic crisis there has been the cryptocurrency Steem. Venezuelans have been able to earn crytocurrency and use it to pay for food, clothing, and shelter.

A USD is worth 10 Venezuelan Bolivars. An SBD is worth 3.5 USD. That means a single SBD is worth 35 Bolivars. In the country where an engineer might only earn $20 USD per MONTH the ability to generate a monthly salary with 1 good post or several smaller posts marks a unique life support.

I’ve asked some folks from Venezuela to speak to us at 11am EST. that’s 3hrs from this post being released (1500 UTC). I’d love it if you can join us, meet some of the Venezuelans that are on the platform and sharing in their home country, and learn about their struggles and various perspectives on how we got there.

Steem Impact Forum- Venezuela

I’m excited to announce a great forum for you tomorrow Saturday May 5th. I’m calling this a Steem Impact Forum and tomorrow’s focus is on Venezuela. As you may not know Venezuela has been having a pretty rough go economically. This is somewhat surprising because of their vast oil reserves, but seems related to the function of their government, malfeasance, mismanagement, and some likely foreign disaster capitalism.

One bright ray of hope for many Venezuelans has been Steem. Some folks have been able to earn a few sbd on a post and sometimes dozens. In a country that pays engineers $16 a month this is an extraordinary gift to the common people.

I wanted to shine both a spotlight on the hardships that Venezuelans face and also talk about how Steem is finding a place helping lift folks out of poverty and helping them eat and find shelter.
