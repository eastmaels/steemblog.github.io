How I setup my mining rig and chose the Radeon 470



So, I have 7 GPUs.  1 is a Radeon Sapphire Nitro Radeon 460 (4GDDR5).  One is a MSI Radeon 480 (4GDDR5).  I have 5 XFX Radeon 470 (4GDDR5).




I bought a 460, 470, and 480 to test out which card made the most sense financially.


I’m running Claymore’s AMD ZEC miner 8.  


Here’s my config file-




-zpool stratum+tcp://equihash.usa.nicehash.com:3357


-zwal 12r76wXi9jznKFGKP8tTDyGqXqT66gwJaL


-zpsw (wouldn’t you like to know)


-allpools 1


-i 6




As you can see, I’m using NiceHash.  It’s paying me in bitcoin, which I’m currently converting to Steem.  I’ll start a full 24 hour cycle at 6pm tonight.  So, I’ll use that as a guage to figure out how much it’s actually making.  NiceHash flucuates all over the effing place so it’s really hard to guage.




So, my 460 drew 45-95 watts of power and pulled in 83 H/S.


My 470 drew 120 watts of power and pulled in 175 H/S.


My 480 drew 150 watts of power and pulled in 190 H/S.




The 460 has literally half the computational cores of the 470 and 480.  Also, @cryptos told me the 470 would be much stronger if I switched to a different coin to mine.  So, I went with the 470s.


 After I have a profit baseline I’ll start messing around with other mining approaches.  When the Hardfork comes on Dec 6 and we switch over to equihash I might actually trying mining Steem and skip the whole Nicehash thing.  We’ll see.  I make between $8-12/day on Nicehash and spend about $5.50 on electricity.  So, squeezing more profit out seems important.


I should also note that I’ve overclocked my GPUs some.  I’m using Sapphire Trixx to do it.  So, if you put stock cards in and don’t quite get those numbers that’s why.
