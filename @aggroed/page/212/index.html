Using STEEMIT to build a youtube following in short order: How I got 222,000 views on youtube from a fresh account in 14 days



To date I’ve put together a total of 14 videos and I’ve put them on youtube.  Today they had a hit total that continues to increase throughout the day- 126,296 130,476 (2:30pm) 136,341 (4:22) 139,201 (5:51) 143,123 (6:45), 147,714 (9:03), 156,563 (10:16) 162,674 (12:53am, final count for 13 days), 213,281 (11:00am 9/20/16)  221,891 (3:45 9/20)hits.  The first video went up September 6th.  The most recent was last night. 


Making these videos was a very small time investment compared to writing the blog posts themselves.  The blog posts take several hours to compile all the relevant pictures and stories that I want to cover. The videos themselves are typically 5-20 minutes just talking over what I already wrote.  Because I’ve already done the research and know the material I can talk pretty off the cuff about what I wrote about.  Most important the audience doesn’t seem to mind.  


The other thing it does is provide a reference page for the article.  News today doesn’t have much that it falls back on for it’s opinions or analysis.  It’s hard to judge where the story came from, who wrote it, and what the purpose is.  Having both details, analysis, and the references allow people to verify a thesis statement for themselves based on what you found.  So, far it’s been pretty popular.  


I didn’t think that what I was doing would take off any where near this quickly, but so far it has.  I think it’s a combination of talking about things are interesting, providing a perspective and analysis on the work that was done, and trying to be respectful while interacting with the audience have all helped.


Creating the videos couldn’t possibly be easier.  I bought a $100 USB Blue Yeti Microphone from best buy.



Then I purchased a 2 year license to Screen Cast-O-Matic for $30.



I plugged in the mic to a USB port, opened the Screen Casting software and hit record and then I started talking over my blog post.  I wouldn’t read line for line, but I’d discuss in a less formal way the things that were on the blog.  I’d go to each picture.  If I included links I’d do that too.


When the recording was done I’d hit save and choose the .flv extension since that’s what youtube wants to use anyway.  I’d upload to youtube, and then I spread around to a few friends and pages on facebook to get it started.  I was talking about things that those pages and people liked and they were happy to share.




What am I going to do with a small and growing youtube audience?  I don’t know.  Share more stories.  Start a newscast.  Start a fake-news cast.  I don’t know.  If I ever crowd source a project having a group of people that habitually watch and like what I put out would be helpful.  Who knows what will come of it.  At the moment it’s fun and since I’ve already written the blog it literally takes about 20 more minutes when I’ve already spent a few hours writing to get the word out another way.


Lastly, I found that it’s easier to get people to view things on youtube.  The audience there is millions.  STEEMIT is still infant at less than a 100,000.  It’s just much easier to spread the word on other media platforms, but it’s still great to build the story here first.
