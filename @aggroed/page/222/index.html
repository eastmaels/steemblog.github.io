Rant against mining in crypto currencies and precious metals backed currencies




Everyone knows and loves Bitcoin.  Well, maybe not everyone.  Some conspiracy theorists out there think it’s a way to move everyone to cashless society where every single transaction is monitored by the controllers.  @matrixdweller did a post on this yesterday!  Welcome to the party bud!


Leaving that aside for a little bit my real beef with cryptos that have mining is that they are inherently undemocratic.  Ok, so the first guys that screw around with it can use real simple computers and get a few coin.  As the process is harder you need more serious hardware to crank out the code to get your coin.  By the end you have server farms running day and night mining bitcoin.



Do you know who can afford to run bitcoin mining operations in third world countries?  Rich people.  That’s it.  I get it. You’re a nerd and your nerd bro can buy some mining hardware and crank out code too, but what are you going to do in your basement compared to banks that invest in buildings full of servers.


So, the only way to get a substantial amount of Bitcoin without doing labor or selling goods for it is to be rich or to be the creator, but let’s face it, that kid probably had a phenomenal background and a host of private schools as a backdrop to be able to build a program like that… so to be that creator you probably had to come from wealth.


Ok, well downer Aggroed, what should we do instead.  Your first guess is “We need a gold backed currency.”  Hold up there too genius.  



Who has money to have gold?  Rich people.  We have a shitty fiat currency backed by the US military, and secondarily backed by the petro dollar, which at the moment seems to be going to shit.  The two alternatives that are presented seem to be bitcoin and gold backed currencies.  The current wealthy class wins in both of those scenarios.


So, what do I propose.  People are the real value.  You can’t eat gold.  If there was a planet made of gold but no one to get it or trade it there would be no perceived value to it.  The only real thing of value is life.  Your soul.  Your labor.  Animals.  Their flesh and their labor.  That’s actual wealth.


So, I propose this.  Issue currency from a central bank to people.  The loan is interest free but the money decays over a finite lifespan so as to not lead to infinite inflation.  All people earn the exact same amount for being a living human.  Then you can use that currency to live a modest life or earn more by all the normal shit one does in an economy.


I have a basic working model of it.  I call it PALCoin.  It stands for peace, abundance, liberty, and Pal of course means friend.  Don’t bother telling me it’s not a beautiful website or the most robust software ever.  I know, I built the whole thing for under $300.  That said, when the time is right and I’m actually prepared for it I could launch an all digital fiat backed currency that doesn’t require mining and doesn’t keep the rich as the rich.


Rant over.  You may now go back your regularly scheduled gold vs cryto debates.




Oh, and this goes for you too STEEMIT.  People are the value.

The Hard Times Series- Apocalypse Soup



Pre-Apocalypse Soup, easy-


1.  Get in BMW


2.  Drive to Whole Foods


3.  Purchase soup


4.  Drive home


5.  Serve Soup




Pre-Apocalypse Soup, Medium-


1. Get in BMW


2. Drive to Whole Foods


3. Purchase vegetables and broth


4.  Drive home


5.  Cut tomato, roast until color darkens


6.  Prepare tomato paste for sauce, use juice for broth


7.  Saute onions and garlic separately add them in


8.  Cut vegetables, and huck into soup


9.  Blend soup into cream.


10.  Serve after 15-20 minutes


11.  Remove extra soup, and pressure cook into 1 quart vessels




Post- Apocalypse Soup


1.  Check for flying snappers (mutated ravens/buzzards)


2.  Head on the roof and fiddle with the Solar Panel


3.  Once power is reestablished take radioactive water from the deep well and run through 4 year old Ion Exchange Chromatograph looted from university.


4.  Triple distill water removing top and bottom fraction each time.  Water can be temporarily stored in the jug, but the jug needs to make it’s way to The Pit.


5.  Venture into camouflaged aquaponics underground walipini


6.  Pick vegetables, pray replacements come quickly


7.  Reattach wooden handle to sharp blade using another section of couch leather and improvised twine


8.  Cut tomatoes, save some of soup and some for sauce


9.  Imagine yourself sauteing garlic and onions


10.  Smash tomatoes with a hammer, put through year old coffee filter.


11.  Wait 2 hrs for filter to finish draining


12.  While waiting setup fire.  Don’t make too large and call attention to it.


13.  Chop the shit out of everything to make it as nutrient dense as possible


14.  Once juice is ready cook juice and vegetables all once.  


15.  Continue to ration out salt and pepper from salvaged stock.


16.  Enjoy soup, pretend family comes home.






—


This is a Walpini.  Image source.



Walpinis are a real thing.  They are underground greehouses.  It’s normally cheaper to build and run than a greenhouse.  In the summer the earth keeps it cool.  In the winter the earth keeps it warm.


When the hard times come I hope my underground Walpini is done.  I also want to do aquaponics in it.  Basically I want to get a deep swimming pool in my back yard.  The deep end is for fish.  The shallow end is for vegetables.  The fact that it’s underground and hopefully with a glass or plastic top means the water won’t completely freeze over in winter.  


Here’s a link to aquaponics.  Grow fish, they put nutrients in the water.  Grow vegetables they take nutrients out of the water and give back clean water to the fish.  Put in fish food, get back vegetables.

Hard Times Series- "Jasmine Come Out"



“Jasmine COME OUT!” Sissy Rick shouted.  ”I told you yesterday I’d be back.  Well here I am!”


Jas looked through the slots in her windows.  Sissy Rick was in that pink sweat stain’d shirt.  Where’d he find those sunglasses since the lights went dark anyway?  Musta had them ‘fore it went dark.  It was clear enough to see him.  Sunlight hit him square midday.


“Come on sweet heart.  This land is needed by the Enclave.  You can join honey.  Just com’ out!”  Fat Jimmy looked like he always did.  Fat.  He was huger ‘fore it went dark, but he was still huge.  His ‘clown pants’ looked big all the time, but now they didn’t fit by a size or two.  He was wearing that red checkered shirt.  He looked like a 4th of July picnic table got fat and grew fat limbs.  I ain’t Fat Jimmy’s sweet heart.  That’s for sure.


“Jas, I’ve known you since the firs’ grade.  You didn’t shoot then.  You don’t shoot now.  Even if you did shoot I know you ain’t got no bullets.  You know how much they cost?” Rick spit on the ground.  ”Look honey.  You got land.  We need land.  We need you too.”  That last part almost sounded sincere.  Trouble is I done know ‘bout you doin’ this to a few other farmers ‘round here.


Also, yes, yes I know what bullets cost.  It cost me all my chickens.  You hear a chicken cluckin’ on my farm?  You hear me cookin’ omelettes today?  Nope.  Were you in town the year I won that shootin’ benefit for the 4-H?  Nope.  I got a surprise for you.  She looked down the barrel of the rifle.  This ain’t but 30 yards.


Rickity Andrew spoke next.  ”Look, why don’t you just come on out and we can talk about this.”  He was old and had a cane.  He was Daddy Cade.  He started walking forward.


“You take one more step I’m gonna draw blood now.  You hear me?  I don’t want this!”  Jasmine shouted.


“Oooooh weeeeeeii.  She got fire for you Pop.”  He slapped his leg and let out a yip.  ”Go ahead then.  We’re gonna walk in.  I took it easy on you yesterday cause we were friend.  Not TODAY!”  He was trying to be intimidating, but ain’t nobody gonna take Sissy Rick serious when it comes to manning up.


“We’re gonna take Bessy.  We’re gonna take them chickens.  Then we’re gonna go!  No one is gonna hurt you, but Jas women and children are starvin’ in the Enclave.  You’r’ tough you’ll be ok!”  Sissy Rick took a look at the chicken coop.  He was thinking about breakfast tomorrow.  He wasn’t stupid, but it took him a minute to figure out with the sun behind the coop that it was quiet and he didn’t see anything.  While he was thinking Fat Jimmy took a step forward.


BOOM.


The sound of the shot rippled across the farm.  Bessy kicked.  Sissy Rick started backing up.  Fat Jimmy started screaming and holding his leg.  Looks like he got his period unexpectedly.  I reckon I got a few days before I know if they coming back or they just gonna let this one slide.


Sissy Rick looked white in the face.  I didn’ even shoot him.  ”You boys owe me some chickens fer’that!”

Hard Times Series- Introduction



My friends and I have spoken for years about a period of time known as The Hard Times.  The Hard Times is a fantasy setting (for Americans anyway) where something happens in the world and everything changes immensely overnight.  If that sounds vague it is and it’s meant to be.  I have a lot of friends that are preppers.  If you watch the show preppers you’ll realize people are doing things for a ton of different reasons.   The first point is YOU ARE NOT PREPARED.  The second point is that no one knows if and when the Hard Times hit and what causes them.



So, we talk about it haphazardly.  It might be a comical discussion where we talk about the Hard Times version that’s a zombie apocalypse. We talk about the Hard Times caused by upheaval in the Middle East and suddenly there isn’t carbon fuels to rely on.  We talk about the Hard Times as some kind of disease outbreak.  There’s lots of ways to create the Hard Times.


In most of these we imagine that cities are not safe places to be in the start at least, government is only partially helpful if at all functioning, and people are forced to rely on local communities to sustain most of what they need.


I was sick for a long time and had a form of writer’s block that went along with it.  I love writing fiction.  So, I’m going to start a series called “The Hard Times” and put different characters in different scenarios.  The world will change.  The cause of the problems will change.  The standard storyline will be some kind of survivor figured out how to make it past the initial happening.  What’s their world look like now?


The series is called “The Hard Times” and I’m making the keyword hardtimes.  If you feel like writing in this environment feel free.  It’s a broad base and it could be fun to play off one another’s characters.

Oath of Enlistment changes- another example of the Mandela Effect



It seems as though all aspects of life are different in the Mandela Effect world.  


Here’s a link to the Army’s Oath of Enlistment page.




Here’s the Oath itself:


 I, _, do solemnly swear (or affirm) that I will support and defend the Constitution of the United States against all enemies, foreign and domestic; that I will bear true faith and allegiance to the same; and that I will obey the orders of the President of the United States and the orders of the officers appointed over me, according to regulations and the Uniform Code of Military Justice. So help me God.” (Title 10, US Code; Act of 5 May 1960 replacing the wording first adopted in 1789, with amendment effective 5 October 1962). 




However, it appears a group of people also remember “uphold, protect, and defend” instead of “support and defend.”




I haven’t spent hours digging on this, but Smith and Wesson runs this ad.  Now, the challenge is always that when there is “reality residue” that there is the chance this is the cause rather than the symptom of the Mandela Effect.  To me though, why would Smith and Wesson run an ad that specifically recites a Mandela Effect version of the Oath of Enlistment.  It would make sense if it was the Oath, but now it seems off.



Any vets out there?  What do you remember?
