Have you met me yet?- Benjamin Fulford

https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/1306/0683/products/have_you_met_me_yet_shirts_1024x1024.jpg?v=1470710756

https://benjaminfulford.net/

Ben Fulford is a journalist and a blogger that writes stories on world events. He was a former Forbes Magazine contributor before he moved to Japan and started covering stories from there. His father was a Canadian politician. Ben has a weekly series that he posts on his website that gives a high level perspective of world events. Some of the things he’s written have come true with remarkable accuracy. The Asian Investment Bank becoming a $50B thing is one of them. Some of the things he’s written have been flat wrong like when he predicted the wrong Pope would emerge victorious.

Discernment

I’m not advocating Ben a sole source of truth. I am advocating Ben as a wonderful place to find stories an headlines that you won’t find elsewhere. He has since described his acceptance into what he calls the “White Dragon Society.” It would appear to be a number of secret societies and possibly criminal gangs that have decided to come together “to help fix the planet.” He now puts out talking points and stories naming sources from within the vatican, CIA, Japanese underground, and other less frequently publicly mentioned groups. He shares a novel perspective, but I wouldn’t read it thinking everything will turn out to be true.

What does he cover

He’ll talk about Khazarians… a lot. They are a group of people that worshipped Baphomet (the goat headed God) and were a lying and duplicitous group. They did a fair amount of conquering themselves but eventually ran against a stronger nation and were basically forced to give up their Baphomet worship and join some of the other cultures. Instead of that they only gave up Baphomet in public and some picked Christianity, Islam, and Judaism as their public religion, but privately they continued worshipping Baphomet. This tribe continues to operate this way with people placed in all of the major religions.

He’ll talk about ongoing news event, but provide a perspective of who is working with who and why. He’ll mention skirmishes that are happening both with soldiers and with economics. He’ll describe the back drop of world politics. He’ll sometimes go into the religious beliefs of the people he’s describing to help clarify how they got to their current view point.

Why I like him the most

His pieces usually end the same way. Something to the effect of: if we work together we can kick out the criminal elements, grow the economy in a meritocracy, share world power, and end environmental destruction along with poverty. There are others in the world that I’m not a huge fan of that share great stories and perspectives of what’s wrong, but then suggest really dumb conclusions going forward. I’m not sure everything he says is true, I do think he believes what he writes, and I like what he’s working towards.

Also too, the world makes more sense to me with some of his world view mixed in. People aren’t bad in general, but there are leaders all throughout the world that are and have become severely compromised as a result. The world being run by a handful of sadistic people with bad intentions and tons of wealth and power makes more sense than the world being run by kind but inept people.

Rick and Morty and the best scientific joke on quantum I've heard in 20 years.

https://images.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.flickeringmyth.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2016%2F07%2Frick-and-morty-600x330.jpg&f=1

Rick and Morty has to be one of the greatest cartoons of all times. If you’ve been living in your bunker for the past few years and just emerged you may not have heard of it. That said it’s incredibly creative.

The show is a riff off of Back to the Future.

https://images.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.silverdoctors.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2014%2F03%2Fback-to-the-future-.jpg&f=1

But instead of Doc being just sorta out there he’s now a bit on the psychopath side. Rick uses Morty as a human shield. While it’s obvious he loves his grandson he’s also not opposed to ripping him a new one and sharing his opinion of Morty’s brain power.

The show dives into the concept of the multiverse. Rick isn’t just Rick. He’s Rick C-137. It’s as if all the possible realities have been mapped out and they know exactly which one they are from. In the season 2 opener Rick, Morty, and Summer are ripped out of their dimension into a timeless vortex.

Here’s the full episode- http://www.dailymotion.com/video/x3atrno

Around the 4:45 mark he says “Those are Schrodinger’s cats. Actually I assume they both are and are not just like us!” That line had me in tears.

Schrodinger’s cat

This takes a leap into understanding our universe from a lens of quantum mechanics. Schrodinger was a Jewish German scientist that was working on understanding the nature of the atom during WWII trying to figure out how to capture the energy of an atom. One of his conclusions was that sub atomic particles can exist simultaneously as both waves and particles. They exist as both until you look at what it actually did, and then you can figure out if it worked as a wave or a particle.

To demonstrate this he came up with a pretty crazy WWII demented story about putting a cat in a box with acid on a timer. Then you shut the box. The timer doesn’t have an actual set time it’s determined to go, so if you want to know if the cat’s alive or not you have to write an equation that includes both possibilities that he’s alive or dead. You don’t know the answer until you lift up the box.

Sorry to explain the joke

But it’s an amazing piece of science and humor. You have to think about the world in time, then separate the world into multiverses, one of those multiverses has to include no time and be unstable for the situation to have drama, and then you have to make pretty sophisticated jokes about quantum mechanics. It’s one thing to crack a joke about a cat, but it’s another to understand it deeply enough to joke that they are simultaneously the cat in question and not. That’s a very rich understand ing and part of why I love that show so much.

The Two Best Documentaries on the Current State of the Law

https://steemitimages.com/0x0/https://images.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.bywayofbicycle.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2013%2F03%2Flaw.jpg&f=1

So, I owe a huge part of my awakening to a simple movie called “Ungrip.” I don’t remember the name of the internet friend that suggested that I go watch it, but the fellow owned a place in NH, had guns, owned his own smoke shop, and stock piled silver and gold with his extra cash. He was cool as hell and I think he was ex-military. So, anyway, I figured, what could go wrong? It’s just a movie!

Little did I know that just by asking a few really simple questions of the legal system things start falling apart. It feels to me like there are so many little transparencies that are stacked on top of what is real. It becomes very difficult to tell what is real and what is not. What laws apply to me? Which laws are “Pretend Laws” (yes that’s a thing)? So, a great starting point to gently slide your way down the rabbit hole is this incredible documentary called Ungrip. It follows the path of a gentle Canadian as he tries to figure out how to unhook himself from aspects of control that didn’t suit him.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tScuHwVtRcY

The second movie I’d suggest is this odd little Australian flick called “What the FUQ: Frequently unanswered questions.” After reading a bunch of material (much of it comes from Anna Von Reitz- http://annavonreitz.com/) I slowly came to terms with the fact that what I think of as “The Government” is actually a private corporation that’s been in existance since 1871 masquerading as a public government when it’s actually a private government services corporation. That’s a lot to mentally and emotionally take in. So, I like this story because it’s just a guy trying to import a car. Nothing outrageous, but during that journey he discovers that the government he’s dealing with is actually a corporation and how he can respond to it.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=umVj5XQYAi8

It’s 3 hours of your life that can help you understand the nature of your surroundings in very plain and easy to understand English. You’ll probably hate me at first if you haven’t gone down this path at all, but about a year from now you can thank me then.
