STEEMIT TIPS- You can make a lot of money by reading other people's posts. Closing in on 700 followers!

https://media1.giphy.com/media/2u11zpzwyMTy8/giphy.gif?response_id=5921944af8d760e4e8ffa7bb

Hold on! There’s no mechanism in Steemit that let’s you get paid just for reading! How can reading a post earn me anything?

I’m glad you asked!

Your goal as an author is provide a service to your audience. The service has multiple parts.

  1. You provide entertainment, which is one of the most heavily valued services in the world!
  2. You provide information. You are relating something someone ought to know, but doesn’t, and won’t learn it unless you share it with them.
  3. You provide a unique perspective. Your stories in your voice with your character are truly unique. Hearing a similar story from you with that vantage can inform my own life.

Reading good stories helps you provide those services

So, go ahead and read some blog posts that are making traction. Some of the posts that make traction are trash being upvoted by bots and friends, some are probably too technical for you to follow in their foot steps, and some are really great pieces of writing that can help and inspire you. Learn from what good steemers are doing so you can find ways to riff off of their success. Hell, the style of this post is straight based of authors on here who I learned from (looking at you @kaylinart).

Learn what your audience cares about

When you write your story try to think about who the audience member is that you’re appealing too. If you have to imagine what they are like you may not actually know much about who is following you. Go lurk on some followers feeds and see what interests them. You may want to spend extra time looking at who has a lot of Steem Power in your followers and cater to them somewhat. True, it’s kinda selling out if you’re tweaking your posts to cater to wealth, but if the message aligns with the wallets then you’re just monetizing the message you stand for. That’s just good business and Steem in many ways can act like a business.

Summary

Go read! Read the best posts. Read your followers posts. Read the wall of people that you want to follow you. Find overlap, improve your writing, and get ‘Riggity rich son!’

The best and worst advice on my wedding day

https://images.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.sharonronan.co.uk%2Fimg%2Fs%2Fv-3%2Fp1440663586-4.jpg&f=1

My wife and I are approaching 15 years of marriage. I’m in my late 30s we got married in our early 20s. We live in New England… it’s basically unheard of.

Anyway, before I got married a friend of mine who was a few years older than me mentioned the best advice I’d received on the actual wedding. It wasn’t logistical. It wasn’t about the whole thing. It’s also ironic to me because a few years later we helped his wife pack up and leave over his infidelity . But the advice was really simple and truly beautiful.

Freeze a moment in time

All he said was… “It’s an amazing and beautiful thing to watch your wife walk down the aisle. Take special care to remember that moment.”

My wife and I ended up getting married near the ocean by a hill. My wife actually chose to walk down the hill with all the switch backs. I got to watch her walk down the aisle at me for literally a minute as she stepped closer. My memory for things like this isn’t amazing, but because of my friend’s advice my brain has a stored reel in there with a title of “Best friend, long walk, wedding.” I play it in my head when I do reiki and I’m trying to pump as much love through my heart as possible.

So, to all of you out there soon to be married. I’d share this simple advice. Stop your brain. Roll camera. And store that long walk down as your heart beats faster!

And then there’s this moment!

I called my grand parents who lived in Florida and couldn’t travel to the wedding. It was about an hour before the ceremony. I tell them “Hey, you mean so much to me. You’ve met my bride and welcomed her. I wanted you to be part of this day, so, I love you both and at least you can participate a little this way!”

My Grandfather stopped, paused, and said “I don’t think you should get married today. It’s a lot easier to say ‘No’ now than it is an hour from now.” I think all the blood must have withdrawn from my face. What a weird time to share that! It’s not like we eloped. He had a few months to say it… oh well… “Can you put my Grandmother on the phone?”

I heard her sweet voice come on the phone… and then she decided to channel some 1950s book on relationships… “You’re a man now. As the man you have to take control of this relationship. You’re the sole head of house, and for that you have to be strong.” It’s like she fast forwarded to now, really bought into Handmaid’s tale, and then went back in time to deliver that marriage advice (or more simply, she just listened to the standard advice of the day).

Marriage is the hardest and most likely best thing you’ll do with your life

I tend to steer people away from marriage at first. If just that little bit of negativity can steer them away then they probably weren’t ready for it. If they stand firm then I switch to highly supportive. Marriage is hard. It’s hard work. In some ways it gets harder as two people living together inevitably hurt one another. Those things build up if they aren’t addressed. Hopefully, you have good role models to learn good and effective ways of communication. At least then you have a chance that after you hurt your other that you can mend.

Jacque Fresco of the Venus Project has died

https://images.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=http%3A%2F%2Fi0.wp.com%2Fwww.activewirehead.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2015%2F01%2FJacque-Fresco.jpg&f=1

A futurist and thought leader has died recently. Jacque Fresco, creator of the Venus Project has died May 18th, 2017 in Sebring FL. This will sadden many people who followed his work looking at one possible iteration of society.

I liked a lot of what he had to say, but ultimately came away from his main thought, namely the resource based economy, as a giant give away to bad people. Let me explain this as a lot of folks seem to really like his ideas.

Zeitgeist

A lot of people learned about Jacque in the movie Zeitgeist. Zeitgeist goes through a couple of reasonably accurate rounds of problems that exist in modern culture.

  1. Religions seem to be based on the same narrative, which is actually a celestial story.
  2. Money is created out of thin air by banks to the detriment of the world
  3. False flag terrorist attacks prove that bad people are in power
  4. We need a new system that eliminates the creation of money and simply aggregates and trades in resources.

The problem

He had me until 4. Right now the Fed has to print money and then trade that money to horde resources. In a resource based economy governments would simply control the resources. I don’t like what governments do most of the time now. Imagine if they had even more control over every aspect of the country.

a different solution

A real reason I like crypto currencies is that I don’t think there are too few currencies in the world. I don’t think there are enough. We also don’t have enough outside of teh 167 governments that control 7.2B people. A few more choices… especially those not backed by war, threat of war, or constant violence and media manipulation would be swell.

So, I’m sad that he passed, but I’m glad we didn’t just turn over all our stuff to governments… that would make things worse. That said, some of his city planning work was pretty cool.
