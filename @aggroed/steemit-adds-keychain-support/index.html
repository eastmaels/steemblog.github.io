Steemit adds keychain support

WOOT! Finally! This baby has been merge ready for a few months, and it’s finally here! OK! Great, so what is keychain and why should we care?!?

Keychain is:

It’s a browser extension for google chrome, brave, and mozilla firefox. It saves your keys locally. After you have put in your keys you can interact with steem based websites very easily. To login to a site that’s keychain compatible you literally just type in your name. Done.

Giving keys

Giving keys to websites is stupid. It’s like ordering a pack of gum at CVS and handing them your bank login and password. Why… why would we do this?

Now Steemit joins the list of tools that allow keychain to sign transactions. No more giving away your keys!

Websites

Chrome/Brave - https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/steem-keychain/lkcjlnjfpbikmcmbachjpdbijejflpcm

Mozilla- https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/steem-keychain/

We have a little home for it on the Steem-Engine discord. So, if you’re a dev looking to add it to your site so that anyone on steem can easily use it without fears taht you’re going to take their shit then join us here: https://discord.gg/MbCVbej

Master Password

You don’t have to do this, but I suggest the following. After you have installed keychain put in your master password. It will automatically break that down into the posting (social), active (wallet), and memo key, AND IT WON’T SAVE YOUR MASTER PASSWORD.

Steem-Engine Tokens

Oh, yeah, and as an aside you can check out your steem-engine tokens in this baby. So, if you’ve bought some JJM, or saved up some orbs or DEC from Splinterlands you can see how much you have right in the extension, send them, or check a brief history.
