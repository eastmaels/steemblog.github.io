@cryptos got me GPU mining. Let's look at the money for that.



In part 1 I talked about the profitability of ASIC devices as far as I can tell.  In part 2 I’m going to talk about the profitability of GPU mining.


I started mining with the old video card in my computer to see if I even had the skills necessary to do it.  I had a GEFORCE GTX 950.  This was a pretty decent card like 4 years ago.  Anyway, I tried mining with it.  I got 20 sols/sec with my CPU using the nicehash miner, but I couldn’t get my graphics card to mine.  So… I upgraded.


I purchased windows 10.  I also got 2 Radeon 470 4gig ddr5 cards (my gaming pc can take 2).  The cards cost me $170 each.  Windows 10 was $120.


I started trying the nicehash miner, but it kept crashing my computer.  So, I switched to claymore’s miner.


I’m now rocking 155 and 155 H/s on my two GPUs.



So, right now those make 0.0025 btc per day on nice hash, which is $1.86.  The computer overall is pull 453 watts at the wall.  The GPUs themselves are pulling about 120 watts each.  I leave my computer on all day, so for the moment I’m going to discount the 313 watts that are wasted by me anyways by leaving the computer on.




12024.19/1000= $0.54


So each of my graphics cards is making $1.32/day or $482/yr.


Now, let’s pretend I have a 6 GPU machine.  I’ll assume it still needs 300 watts at the wall and then there are 6 hungry GPUs eating 120 watts at the wall too.


that’s 920 watts.  That’s $4.20/day in electricity.  They make $11.16/day in BTC.  


In a year that rig could make $2540 profit.  You’re going to lose some to Claymore’s development fees, and conversions and Nicehash fees, but take off 3% of $2540 and it’s still a good chunk of change.  




That said there are a bunch of variables.  Claymore’s speed changes.  Nicehash payout changes.  Value of ZEC goes up/down.  Cost of electricity changes. etc.




But at least on the outside it looks like you can GPU or ASIC mine now if you have the right equipment.




-Aggroed
