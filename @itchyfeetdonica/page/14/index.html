#230 Kuala Lumpur Skyline üá≤üáæ | ÂêâÈöÜÂù°‰∏ÄÁû•

##
This is the Kuala Lumpur skyline viewed from Titiwangsa Recreational Park. I strolled around the huge park for a few hours with my mom. It was the golden sunset hour when we reached this spot, and I found it’s a great point to look at those landmarks in the city such as the famous Petronas Twin Towers and the tall tower Menara Kuala Lumpur. The clouds, lake and reflections added more layers to the beauty.

Titiwangsa Recreational Parks spreads across an area of 95 hectares, more than half of which is a big lake. When it was under British Rule in history, this area was full of tin mining activities. Later on, it was cleaned up and developed into a green space. When we walked around the lake garden, we saw people jogging, cycling, boating, kayaking and having picnic with families or friends. This green lung in the gigantic hectic city seemed to be a popular place to relax a bit.

##
这是从蒂蒂旺莎湖滨公园拍到的吉隆坡一角。英治时期，公园所在的这块地方曾是锡矿开采区。后来被清理并开发成公园绿地，面积95公顷，其中一个巨大的湖占地超过一半。我带着妈妈在这个大公园里逛了一圈，看到很多人在慢跑、划船、野餐…… 这块绿肺似乎是吉隆坡这个繁忙的大都市里休闲放松的一个好去处。

#227 5-Minute Freewrite: Shining Like Gold üåü | ÈáëÈó™Èó™ÁöÑÊïÖ‰∫ã

##
Greetings everyone! I’m joining the portrait photo contest with the head picture I took in Sri Lanka. And here’s another reason I chose the photo: I saw @mariannewest’s Tuesday prompt for the 5 Minute Freewrite is GOLD. It reminds me of this cute little girl who dressed in goldish and stood in front of some goldish saree (see the second photo below). Plus her smile in the sun, together with her sweetness and intelligence made her shine like gold. :)

It’s my first time participating this Freewrite challenge, and I’m not sure if the paragraphs I’m writing in 5 minutes would meet the requirement. But it’s fun to try. If you haven’t known about this awesome daily writing challenge, feel free to check it out and join in too!

So I met this GOLD girl during my travels in the tea country in Sri Lanka. I took a train from the capital city and had a few stopovers on my way. Her hometown is one of the villages I stopped by. Her mother was running a small guest house which was part of their own home. She was the youngest daughter in the family, and welcomed me when I arrived at their hillside home from the train station. She helped me register my passport info and showed me around their house.

Her mother offered me some sweets and the local tea (of course ;)), and went on with her housework. So the little girl and I had a very pleasant conversation full of laughter, about her studies at school, the famous local tea, their village, my travels in her country and other random stuff. I know, seems I could enjoy talking with a 7 year-old as well as a 70 one. ;) On the wall of their living room, lots of her certificates of merit and event photos from school were displayed. Apparently she’s a smart and talented girl. And it’s proved soon.

After I checked into my room, I found that the socket on the wall didn’t work for charging. So I walked downstairs to tell the girl’s mother about it. Before she replied, this GOLD girl came to the rescue. She grabbed a pencil and said:”Follow me.” After a few attempts with the magical pencil, the socket worked again. Sweet, wasn’t it?

##

##
Later, when I stepped out of the house for a walk in town, I saw her helping her mom put up the golden saree for drying. Hearing my steps, she turned around and smiled to me, standing in the sun, shining like gold.
