##
After crossing the Strait of Gibraltar from the Spanish mainland, I reached the port of Ceuta. So was I in an African town already? Not really. Technically I was still in European Union.

Ceuta is not a town of any African country, but a Spanish autonomous city on the north coast of Africa. It’s sharing a land border with M’diq-Fnideq Prefecture in Morocco. Together with another Spanish enclave Melilla on the northern shores of Morocco’s Mediterranean coast, they form the EU’s only land borders with Africa.

##

##
坐轮渡从欧洲经直布罗陀海峡来到非洲后，这Ceuta是落脚的第一个小城。虽然脚踩在非洲大陆的土地上，这里却是西班牙的属地。

Ceuta和另一座西属小城Melilla嵌在摩洛哥以北的地中海岸，共同组成了欧盟和非洲的陆上边界。就像法国最北端和英国隔海相望的加莱港一样，这里也是非洲移民难民奔向新天地的重要通道。两地也都是高墙林立、戒备森严。

本打算直接往南走，跨越国界去摩洛哥，但下船后看到这个小城还挺迷人，正好天气也给力，晴空万里、碧蓝如洗，似乎都在召唤我的双脚，就决定先在这里逛逛再走。这些照片就是瞎逛时拍下的。

##

##

##
It was a gorgeous sunny day. Blue sky and no clouds. The original plan was to go on to the south to cross the border, but the beautiful weather and cityscape attracted me to explore a bit here. So I strolled a couple of hours in this small town and snapped some shots. The beach was nice and quiet with only a few sunbathers. If I was not mistaken, there were much more sea urchins in the crystal clear water than humans . ;)

##

##

##

##
Due to its strategic location, Ceuta had been an important naval base since Carthaginian and Roman times. Its early form of fortification since the 5th century was strengthened by the Portuguese in the 1540s.

Today, part of the Royal Walls including the ditch and bastions remain intact. After the restoration in recent years, they have been open to the public. I walked around this heritage site oozing with history where the past battle scenes seemed to be replaying.

##

##

Ceuta was actually acquired from Portugal by Spain at the end of the Iberian Union in 1640 when the 2 countries had the same King, and it served as a free port till Spain joined the EU.

Later I saw a large number of Moroccans crossing the border from their country to shop and work here, and then return to Morocco in the evening. I didn’t see anyone climbing the wired fence over the border, but there’s news report about it and the crackdown of attempts to illegally emmigrate to Europe seeking for a better life.

What’s up fellow Steemians! Here are some photos I took last week in Chefchaouen of Morocco. The head image is my entry for the People Photography contest by @worldcapture.

This little girl was feeding a bunch of stray cats at a street corner, and I couldn’t help recording her sweetness. Chefchaouen is a very touristic town as I expected. I named it “another girl” because it reminds me of the similar scene I captured before in Turkey. I posted the “Syrian Girl Feeding a Cat” in 2017 when I just joined Steem:

##
Greetings everyone! I’m still traveling in Morocco and sharing some shots snapped at the place I stayed in Fez.

Full of old-world charm, Fez is often referred to as Morocco’s cultural capital, and is famous for its walled medina with medieval Marinid architecture. From the 13th to the 15th century, the country was ruled by Marinid, a Sunni Muslim dynasty of Zenata Berber descent.

The elaborate structures, ornate decorations and intricate details in this place are just amazing. They look different from the previous few riads I stayed in other towns of Morocco. Though it’s not my kind of style, I was impressed and kept wondering how much time and labor had been devoted into building and restoring this stunning palace.

##
这里分享些我在摩洛哥菲斯古城住处内部拍的照片。菲斯历史悠久，常被称作摩洛哥的文化之都，尤其是旧城，处处散发着古朴神秘的韵味。

这家旅馆就位于旧城，由古老的宫殿改建而成。这些繁复精美的建筑结构和装饰跟之前在其他地方呆过的几家riad(庭院老屋)有所不同，虽然不是我喜欢的风格，但还是很让人惊叹。我边转边拍，不住地感慨，建造和修缮这些杰作该是投入了多少时间和劳力呀。

Inspired by @kus-knee’s Show Me a Sign contest, I’ve digged out an old picture from my phone and will also present you another sign photo freshly taken in my current trip.

##

##
The first picture here was shot in Castro, a city on Chiloé Island in Chile. It’s known for its colorful wooden stilt houses (palafitos) and churches. I loved this quiet and peaceful place that had so many creative and unique architectures to offer.

This sign is of a Chilean style Cevichería restaurant, and oh how I miss the Peruvian ceviches! I had the fresh delicious treat almost every week when living in Chile ~ Qué rico! :)

The simple sign adds a playful touch to the traditional wooden house, and its name and the fish indicate the restaurant’s signature dish, the typical element of Chilotan architecture palafitos, and its location right by the Pacific Ocean.

##

##
Here’s another sign ( actually 2 if you also count the one behind) I just captured when visiting Chefchaouen. It’s a colorful place as well but much more touristic. It’s in the Rif Mountains of northwest Morocco and is known for the old town’s blue-painted buildings.

Still some distance from the Sahara Desert, but this lovely sign did match the blue background and successfully caught my attention.

#247 Only in Australia (1) - the Big Prawn ü¶ê | Êæ≥Ê¥≤ÁâπÊúâÔºà‰∏ÄÔºâ

##
Thanks to the mention by the very talented photographer @lightcaptured, I got to know his 4th #TravelPhotoContest themed Landmarks. So the first thing popping up in my mind is not the super famous landmarks I’ve visited like Great Wall, Machu Picchu, Christ the Redeemer, Eiffel Tower, Leaning Tower of Pisa, the Statue of Liberty, Sydney Opera House, or Moai on the Easter Island🗿(I know, I’m such a typical tourist right? ;p It’s a great idea for a series of Landmark posts though, so I’m listing them here for future ;)), but a huge prawn 🦐 erected in a small town in Australia.

##

##

##
Also, I’m traveling now without access to most of my past camera photos, yet the pictures of this Big Prawn taken with my phone are lying there still (no pun intended). So here they are - a dedication to my lovely memories in Straya 🇦🇺, this amazing country that I will for sure go back one day. :)

##

##
The statue is in Ballina, a town located in New South Wales, serving as a gateway to the popular site Byron Bay. Built in 1989 and reopened in 2013 with a makeover, the Big Prawn is another of Australia’s iconic big things. There are also Big Pineapple, Big Banana, Big Peach… but I didn’t stop to take decent pictures of them.

This Big Prawn is situated adjacent to Bunnings Warehouse on River Street and have attracted road trippers like us.
