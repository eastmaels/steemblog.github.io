#308 Colca Canyon üèî | Ê∑±Ë∞∑È£éÊôØ

##
Colca Canyon is one of the most impressive places that I visited in Peru. It is located in the southern part of the country, and is less visited than some other better known star sights.

The canyon here is claimed to be the deepest one in the world, almost twice as deep as the Grand Canyon in the US. When I was hiking in the canyon, the stunning views and the high elevation kept taking my breath away.

The majestic high mountains and snow-capped volcanoes were shrouded in mist; the crystal clear creek were flowing at the bottom of the deep canyons; and the condors were soaring above my head.

Deep in the mountains, the Inca traditions here are little altered. The locals still cultivate the pre-Inca stepped terraces built into the slopes.

#306 Peruvian Ceviche üáµüá™ üòã | ÁßòÈ≤ÅÂõΩËèú

##
The heat wave here makes me crave for the tasty cold dish ceviche/cebiche. Originated in Peru, this treat has become popular across the Americas, and it’s not difficult to find it in big cities in other parts of the world.

Here I’m sharing a photo of ceviche I had in Arequiba of Peru. I ate the dish almost every week when living in Chile and traveling through Peru, and it’s always an enjoyable experience. :) I’d love to share much much more delicious Peruvian food in future when I find time to go through my pictures.

Ceviche is typically made from raw fish cured in lime or lemon juice, and spiced with chili peppers and other seasonings like onions, cilantro and salt. And in Peru, most ceviche dishes I tried were accompanied by side dishes, such as sweet potato, toasted corns, plantain chips and lettuce. As you can see from my photo, these colorful elements look very pleasant and help open up appetite.

The tangy sour flavor goes so well with the cold fresh fish, plus its healthy dose, it’s such a delight to enjoy in summer time!

##
If you have been to the tropical areas of Latin America, you might have seen this genus of lizard in the wild. I spotted this iguana at the coast on the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico and shot these pictures.

I immediately thought of this fellow when I saw @phortun’s Reptile photo contest. Since he’s based in Riviera Maya and I remember seeing his photos of iguanas. These lizards were quite common sights when I was visiting that region. Does this one look familiar @phortun? ;)

##

##
The genus includes 2 species: the green iguana which is a popular pet and the endangered Lesser Antillean iguana. Green iguanas are native to Central and South America, and their meat is a delicacy on some locals’ dining tables.

While in Puerto Rico where I lived, they pose a threat to farmers by eating a large amount of their crops. Without any predators on the island, they become an invasive species.

These big reptiles boast keen vision which enables them to locate food in long distance and to communicate with other members.

#304 Whirling Up | ÂõûÊóãËàû

##
Here’s a dancing figurine of the Whirling Dervishes, a piece of artwork I found on a street of Istanbul. I watched the dance that day and was impressed by its unique spiritual atmosphere.

The mysterious Whirling Dervishes are a part of the Sufism of Islam. They believe that by performing the Sema dance, dancers can abandon their personal desires and unify with God to achieve the love and wisdom.

As a part of the religious ceremony “Sema”, this whirling dance is originated in the 13th century in Konya of Turkey, and you can find the performance and relevant souveniors in many parts of the country.

I found the dancing quite mesmerising. The Dervishes based their whirling on the left foot, while the right foot gave impetus to the rotation in 360-degree steps. Meanwhile, they opened their arms towards the sky and the earth, symbolizing God’s beneficence from heaven and his love to people.

In my photo here, you can see the dancer’s wearing a tall, conical felt hat (signifying the tombstones of their egos) and a white robe (symbolizing the shrouds of their egos). When I saw this hanging statuette, I felt its white costume go well with the dark background, and the beautiful shape and lines make a good monochrome picture, so I shot it in black and white. It’s my entry to @derangedvisions’ Photography Contest - Black and White. I like his explanation, which might be inspiring for you too:

What is black and white photography? It is more than just desaturating your image and removing all of the color from it. True black and white photography removes all of the distractions that can come with color and reveals the true essence and emotion of a photograph.

##
When I shoot, I try and envision what I want the final image to look like, and the type of feel that I want it to have. Doing so makes me conscious of my lens selection, aperture, shutter speed, angles, back drop, and then I have to think ahead to how the photo is going to be edited as well, so I can make sure that I am telling the story through the lens in the first place when I capture the photo so that it ends up the way I want it to when I am done with the final edits.
