##
I had a delicious meal in a local specialty restaurant in Überlingen, a German city on the northern shore of Lake Constance.

It’s called Schwabenpfännle (the fourth choice on their menu below) - grilled pork fillets with mushroom sauce, spätzle and lettuce salad. Although the yellow egg noodles (Spätzle) themselves didn’t have much taste, they soaked the rich mushroom and meat gravy to offer very satisfying bites.

##

##

##
Except for southern Germany and Austria, Spätzle is also found in the cuisines of Switzerland, Hungary, Alsace, Moselle and South Tyrol. Literally meaning “little sparrows” in German, it is a kind of pasta made with fresh eggs. Spätzle doesn’t only accompany meat dishes, but also act as a primary ingredient in other types of food. For instance, I tried Spätzle in soup when traveling in Hungary and ate it sweet with sugar and cinnamon in Alsace.

#235 Tulipa Cummins üå∑| ÈÉÅÈáëÈ¶ô

##
When I visited the famous Dutch garden Keukenhof during spring time, various types of gorgeous flowers were blooming. So I had a feast to my eyes and camera cards. :) This ravishing fringed tulip in my photo is called Tulipa Cummins. The classic goblet shape and violet purple tint are not uncommon. What caught my attention was its finely incised snow-white edges.

I focused on its extravagantly fringed petals when shooting, since this unique feature made it stand out from other types of tulips. Its ornate fringe comes from mutations in tulips of different categories.

Tulipa Cummins usually bloom at mid to late spring, and they stay vibrant pretty long in the garden and vase.

##
看到@kelvinzhang发了在荷兰库肯霍夫花园拍的绣球花，想起我在那里看到的各种花。

这次分享的是一种叫“康明斯”的郁金香🌷，鲜黄的花蕊、紫红的花瓣和雪白的锯齿状边缘让它从花丛中脱颖而出，成功抓住我的眼球和镜头。

我拍照时聚焦在它别具一格的花瓣末端上，调大光圈虚化了绿叶和后面的几朵花。这种郁金香一般在春天的中期到晚期开花，不管是种在土里还是插在水中，都能持续鲜艳挺长一段时间，好看又好养。

##
Greetings everybody! Following my last post of desert flowers, today I’m presenting another type - the cactus🌵 flower.

I shot these photos off the Pacific Coast of Chile, in Atacama region. As I mentioned in my last post, this area is one of the driest places in the world. Since some parts are between the Andes Mountains and the Chilean Coast Range, the moisture from either the Pacific or the Atlantic Oceans is prevented. So you can imagine how hot and arid it is here.

I was totally burned traveling through the Atacama, but at the same time very impressed by the wild beauty of this vast land and the tenacious vitality of the thriving plants such as these cacti and their flowers.

##
这是我在南美智利的阿塔卡马沙漠地带拍的🌵仙人掌花。上一篇说到这里是地球上最干旱的地方之一，有些区域历史上从来没有过一滴降水。

我跟其他几个来自世界各地的旅行爱好者用了十几天时间穿越了这片沙漠，被晒成黑人。虽然极度干热，蜕皮好几层，但那原始无际的自然美景和人类、动植物在这片荒原上的顽强生存真的很让人惊叹，绝对不虚此行。

##
Hey everyone! Today I’m sharing some flower photos I shot in the Atacama desert region of Chile.

Most part of the Atacama Desert is situated on the Pacific coast of Chile, west of the Andes mountains. This area is one of the driest places as well as the only true desert in the world to receive less precipitation than the polar deserts. The average annual rainfall here is about 15 mm (0.6 in), although some weather stations have never received rain.

##

##
In spite of the extreme conditions of the desert, some flowers like the white one in my pictures are still blooming. This small slim flower might not be as pretty as many other types, but what an extraordinary ability it has to adapt to this harsh environment! I have no idea what it is called. Anyone knows please tell me in the comment. :)

##
Further and further:

##

##

##
今天分享的是我在智利北部的阿塔卡马沙漠地带拍的花。这是地球上最干热的地方之一，整块地区全年平均降雨量只有15毫米，很多地方历史上没有任何降水。

这种细小的白花不如很多其他花朵那么鲜艳饱满，但在这种严酷的沙漠环境下，它们顽强的生命力让我不由自主地拍了好几张。

#232 Spring is in the air! (2) üå∏ | Êò•Ôºà‰∫åÔºâ

##
Greetings everyone! Following my previous post of Spring is in the air!(1) with loads of flower photos I took, here I’m sharing another type of spring flower - cherry blossom. It’s my entry to Qurator’s Photo Friday competition and Flamingirl’s 🌸 FLOWER POWER PHOTO CONTEST.

Do you know an obvious difference between the cherry flower and the plum/peach flower? As you can see from the emoji and my photos, cherry blossoms’ petals have a cleft at the tip, while the other 2 don’t. At least, this is some unique feature that I observe to tell them part. =) Besides, cherry blossoms have longer stems than peach blossoms, and plum blossoms literally have no stems as they grow out from the branch itself.

##

##

##
I found these trees near my home in Belgium during a cycling trip. They are full of gorgeous white to pink blossoms. Cherry blossoms have a short life of around two weeks, and are in full bloom in spring time, so they are a symbol of the spring and the fleeting nature of life.

##

##

##
When I was taking pictures of these flowers a few days ago, the blossoms already started to fall, so the ground under the trees was covered by a carpet of soft petals. One man working in his garden nearby saw me and greeted me: “Hey, are you from Japan? I believe these flowers are from Japan.” :)

I told him I’m not, but cherry blossom trees and their blossoms (Sakura) do play an important role in Japanese culture. Each spring, my friends who live in Japan would show me photos and videos of people enjoying Hanami (cherry blossom viewing) picnic party. During their national flower’s bloom season, Japanese people celebrate this old tradition with friends, families and co-workers underneath the cherry trees.
