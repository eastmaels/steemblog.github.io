##
Hey, what’s up fellow Steemians! What you see in my photos here are part of the Royal Palace of Cambodia in its capital city Phnom Penh. The head image is my entry to the Qurator Photo Friday competition.

今天请大家跟随我的脚步，来看看位于首都金边的一部分柬埔寨皇宫。

##

##
It’s the moment before a tropical shower when the changing sky and clouds casted a dramatic backdrop for these buildings. The girl in the picture was cycling hard to escape the coming storm, and the heavy rain started to pour when I walked fast till a restaurant by the square.

The fried chicken restaurant franchise is called Bonchon Chicken. They are commonly seen in Southeast Asia and the USA, but there’s only 1 store in its birthplace of South Korea. So I ordered a coffee and a Bibimbap while waiting for the rain to stop. I’m not sure if the meal was genuinely Korean, but it tasted great and this place offered a nice view point for the palace and the square.

拍这前几张照片时，正好赶上狂风大作、乌云压城。路上的行人都加快了脚步，那位骑车的妹纸也开始猛踩踏板，拼命想躲过即将来临的大雨。果然，等我一路小跑到前面一家小餐馆时，立刻暴雨倾盆。

而这家叫Bonchon的韩式连锁餐厅的位置和环境都不错，是个看风景躲雨的好地方，值得推荐。这餐馆主打炸鸡，在东南亚和美国见到不少，据说它起源于釜山，在本土却只开了一家，看来是个墙内开花墙外香的典型呀。我不禁想到熊猫快餐、左宗棠鸡、幸运饼干这类吃的，哈哈……

其实开始只打算喝点东西躲个雨，结果雨一直下个不停，奶茶、咖啡都喝完了还没有停的迹象。我都等饿了，只好又点了份石锅拌饭。看上去应该不是韩国人做的，而是统一培训出来的，但味道还可以，咖啡的花也拉得挺熟练~

##
The whole complex covers an extensive area of 174,870 square metres. Its throne hall, defensive wall, Temple of the Emerald Buddha, stupas and towering spires are all typical elements of Khmer architecture with a slight French touch.

##

##
In the mid-19th century, the construction of the palace started after the royal capital was relocated here from Oudong. The site was chosen at the Western bank of the cross division of the two important rivers - Mekong River and Tonle Sap River.

##

##
The Kings of Cambodia have lived in the palace since it was built except for the period of the Khmer Rouge.

Hello my friends, how’s your weekend going? My schedule is pretty full and I’ve been having a neck pain these days, so I’m not writing much today but just posting some photos from my phone.

I’m going to show you a beautiful spot I visited in Salvador, Brazil.

These pictures were shot close to Farol da Barra Lighthouse at the southern tip of the colorful coastal city. Originally built at Santo António Fort in the 1690s, this site has been long attracting visitors together with its charming surroundings. It is open to visitors from 9am to 6pm except for Monday, but you can come enjoy the nice beach, the sea breeze and the scenic views with slender palm trees 🌴 anytime you want.

I loved the brilliant sunset over the lighthouse, and oh how I miss the huge fresh coconuts! 🥥 :)

##
今天通过几张照片向大家展示一下巴西北部古城萨尔瓦多最南端的一处景点。题图里的巴拉灯塔最初建于17世纪90年代，现在它和附近的海滩成为市民和游客休闲放松的好去处。

如果你来到这里，随便走走、吹吹海风、看看日落，都自有一番舒心惬意。还有记得尝试下小摊上又甜又便宜的椰子水，一个巨大的椰子折合人民币只要三块钱！当然如果你也有当地人的爬树本领，直接去摘个椰子也行～

##
This elderly man was walking in front of me in the medina of Tétouan, Morocco. He’s wearing a Fez hat and a traditional long robe djellaba which is commonly seen in the Maghreb region of North Africa.

This type of robe is usually made of either cotton for summer wear or coarse wool for the winter. I’ve frequently seen flocks of sheep when traveling in the surrounding Atlas mountains where the wool is typically obtained.

The baggy hood with a point at the back can protect the wearer from the sun, the snow and rain or the sand blown by strong desert wind.

It’s not a typical street fashion by an urban youth (though in recent years quite a lot of designers have been inspired by these robes to create modern kaftans), but this local man in the traditional wear just blends perfectly with the charming old streets. What do you think?

The head image is my entry to @worldcapture’s People Photography Contest week #24. Below is the photo in original colors joining the @portraitcontest themed Fashion.

##

##
漫步在摩洛哥北部山城得土安的老城区，看到前面这位当地老人，手里提着刚从旁边的菜市场买来的食材，身穿起源本国的北非传统长袍，头戴平顶菲斯圆毡帽，跟周边的老街和谐地融为一体。

这种长袍通常有冬夏两种，夏天穿的由轻薄的棉布做成，冬装的原料是粗糙些的羊毛，来自周边的阿特拉斯山区，我在山里走时常常能看到成群的绵羊和牧羊人。上面连着的宽大尖帽子可以遮阳挡雨、御寒防沙，在这离沙漠不远的气候多变的山区很实用。

不少国际知名的服装设计师，尤其是法国人（摩洛哥曾是法国殖民地），从这类北非传统长袍中汲取灵感，创新出更多款式的现代版长袍长裙，在时装界挺受欢迎。

##
Hey everyone, here’s a meal I had last weekend - Dorado fish with crab risotto and asparagus. It’s my entry for the Tasty Tuesday competition by @qurator.

##
上周末吃的海鲤蟹肉饭，里面还搭配了三种芦笋 - 常见的绿芦笋、见不得光长在地下的白芦笋和口感爽脆的海芦笋。眼前满满一盘子的春天~ :)

##

##
The Dorado fish meat is tender and a bit sweet. It is low on fat and pretty firm in texture. Its mild flavor goes very well with the more salty and rich crab risotto underneath. The crunchy marsh samphire (glasswort, sea bean, pickleweed, picklegrass) and the two types of asparagus add a fresh taste to the dish. You can smell the spring!

The white asparagus has a hint of bitterness and is softer than the green one. It’s also more expensive as a result of the more time-consuming growing method. They have to be buried underground to avoid any sunlight which would turn them green. I personally prefer the grassy green asparagus than the more fibrous white one. What about you? And some call the marsh samphire sea asparagus, so there are 3 types of asparagus in this plate! :)
