##
Unlike most travelers going by land transportation, I reached Torres del Paine National Park in the Chilean part by boat through Rio Serrano. :) And it proved to be a great choice.

After some hiking, I was stunned by this view at sunset - five elegant guanacos grazing at the altiplano steppe, with the magnificent snow mountains as the backdrop. These camelids are native to South America, closely related to their more famous cousin - llama. =)

I also felt lucky to have visited Patagonia during autumn time, as the landscape in the warm tone was surely a feast to eyes!

##
坐船到达智利南部的百内国家公园后，徒步了一截，就见到这番美景。夕阳西下，几匹骆马（相当于草泥马的表弟）在悠闲地吃着草，背后的雪山静静矗立，那种世外桃源般的绝妙景色，很难用语言形容。幸好拍下这张照片，省得我搜肠刮肚来描绘。。。=) 选在秋天去那边也很明智，漫山遍野的暖色调，美不胜收。

##
Red roses are a widely known symbol of love, passion and courage, so they are popular gifts for Valentine’s Day, like the ones I got here. This is my entry for the VALENTINE’S DAY PHOTO CONTEST 2019 by @flamingirl. I post 3 photos from different angles as proof that they are my photoworks. More details can be provided if necessary.

##

##
Here’s a bit history and legend about roses. Earliest roses are known to have grown 35 million-years ago, and from ancient Egyptian tombs, petrified rose wreaths have been unearthed.

In Greek mythology, Aphrodite, Goddess of Love, is said to have created the rose. According to the poet Anacreon, sea foam dripping from her newborn body turned into white roses; thus symbolizing her innocence and purity. Later, her wounded lover Adonis’ blood was shed onto a white rose, and the red rose represented her desire and passion.

The Romans, with their counterpart Goddess of Love - Venus, also adopted the rose as a symbol of love and beauty. Cupid offered a rose to bribe the God of Silence to hush his mother Venus’s affairs. And when he shot arrows at bees which had stung him, thorns grew from the rose stems where the bees were missed.

There’s even The War of the Roses. In this civil war in England in the 15th century, the House of York adopted a white rose, while the House of Lancaster chose a red rose. At last, the winner - Tudor Henry VII created the Tudor Rose by merging his Lancastrian rose with the red rose of his York bride. This Rose is also known as the Rose of England.

I write down the Chinese character of SPRING in front with a typical brush for calligraphy, but I don’t have Chinese ink at home, so I use the black metallic paint instead. It’s not as smooth, but it’s shining which is pretty cool and festive. :)

I feel this type of paint is suitable for making greeting cards (like what I did when DIYing the Christmas card), and red packets are similar to greeting cards in terms of their function and medium, aren’t they?

##
我小手一挥，在红包正面写下个“春”字，因为春节、春回大地嘛，总不能写个“猪”🐖字吧~ 😃 因为家里没有墨水，就用黑色闪光涂料代替了。虽然没那么丝滑好运笔，比较干涩，但有一种bling bling的效果，简直亮瞎眼~ 想想上次DIY圣诞贺卡也是用的这种涂料，反正红包和贺卡不是差不多的东西吗？一个供写字，一个供装钱+写字。。。
