Blockstream Releases New Programming Language Called "Simplicity"

Blockstream developer Dr. Russell O’Connor has released a design for a new programming language at PLAS 2017 that will enable new features to be built into Bitcoin.

Blockstream’s blog goes on to describe, “As an alternative to existing cryptocurrency languages, the sequent-calculus-based Simplicity language offers a few major improvements, the first of which is extended capabilities. One of the problems with Bitcoin Script, Bitcoin’s own programming language, is that a number of opcodes were disabled in Bitcoin’s earliest days. For example, you can add numbers in Bitcoin Script, but you can’t multiply them. O’Connor wanted something more flexible.”

Dr. O’Connor goes on to say, “Simplicity is a low-level, typed, functional, native MAST language where programs are built from basic combinators. Like Bitcoin Script, Simplicity is designed to operate at the consensus layer. While one can write Simplicity by hand, it is expected to be the target of one, or multiple, front-end languages.”

While this will help develop new functionality to Bitcoin, like smart contracts, we’re still a bit off. Currently the project is still a part of the Blockstream R&D division where Dr. O’Connor and crew will work to create an SDK. From there they hope to merge it into the Elements project so other developers will be able to experiment on sidechains.

There’s still a long way to go but this will enable many new features that weren’t possible in bitcoin before. To get a deeper understanding of Simplicity check out their white paper.

Source: https://blockstream.com/2017/10/30/simplicity.html

21.co Pivots to Become Earn.com

21.co is no more. This isn’t the end for one of the best funded bitcoin companies that brought us the The Bitcoin Computer though, in fact, it is just the beginning. Now they will be known as Earn.com which better identifies their new objectives of helping users get bitcoin for their time.

This isn’t without warning though. 21 took a strong pivot over that past year to move away from their prior hardware goals towards a new software future. After launching a platform that allowed users to create a public profile and get paid to respond to messages, they expanded over the past few months to create a Gmail bounce solution that expands on getting paid for your attention.

Now it’s very clear they plan to move forward with this pivot. This is echoed by the fact their old website will forward your views directly to their newly redesigned website and they have changed all of their legacy social networks to earndotcom.

“The idea behind Earn.com is that we’ve built a positive-sum social network, where every notification is an opportunity to earn money. This is quite different from existing social networks, where notifications are often distracting or even downright negative.”

The new network will be built upon a new ERC20 token that they will begin giving out for user’s time. In fact that will be the only way for regular users to gain access to the token directly from Earn. (That being said since they are ERC20 tokens you will be able to exchange them using other exchanges easily.) They will begin by creating 21 million tokens and begin giving them out. As the userbase of Earn.com doubles then the token issuance will be cut in half.

I have to say that I’ve been a 21.co user from their Bitcoin Computer days and even followed them through to have all my personal gmail messages filtered and bounced using their service. As with a lot of users I will miss the old name but can definitely see the value to their new name. Don’t fret if you were also a prior user. All of your usernames and messages will simply be moved the new domain. This reminds me, if you ever need to reach out, feel free to send me a message at https://earn.com/patrickulrich/.

Source: https://news.earn.com/21-co-is-now-earn-com-3e37e50c444f

Airbitz Rebrands Wallet to Edge

Airbitz announced via medium that their beloved Bitcoin wallet would be rebranding to become Edge Wallet.

This rebrand won’t be just in a name though. While highlighting Airbitz’s unique client side security they will now expand the features of the platform to include additional currencies plus a way to seamlessly convert between them and also new tech that will enable lower payment fees.

The new currencies that will be supported out of the gate are Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, Bitcoin Cash, Augur REP, Wings, and Matryx tokens. These won’t be the only coins to gain support either. Since the new wallet is written from the ground up Airbitz built a new modular architecture that will allow it to be able to secure and transact with any blockchain or token through a simple plugin. This will enable developers to contribute their support and see their currency available on the Edge platform.

Finally some new features that will be included is the ability to natively use Shapeshift.io to convert tokens from one chain to the other. This will save users the step of sending the coins to Shapeshift thus reducing the risk of error for conversions. They have also enabled SegWit support for both Bitcoin and Litecoin which should help wallet users by giving them a smaller tx and thus lower fees.

While Edge Wallet is currently in an limited release at the moment but you can request an email invitation by heading here.

Source: https://medium.com/airbitz/airbitz-is-now-edge-461fff6af2d0

Ready for Another Fork?: Bitcoin Silver Is On The Way!

Bitcointalk user Simonsolo dropped a new potential fork to the world yesterday. Yes you heard that right another new fork of bitcoin.

The similarities to Bitcoin Gold don’t end with just the naming either. The Bitcointalk post lists 4 main bullet points such as decentralization, fair distribution, replay protection and transparency all the same as what are listed on Bitcoin Gold’s website. It goes even deeper, they will also share the same proof of work algorithm Equihash which is what’s credited for the decentralization of Satoshi’s vision of “one CPU on vote.”

Who’s with me to do Bitcoin Bronze in January?

Potential Amazon Bitcoin Announcement?

Back in September, The James Altucher Report stated that Amazon could make an announcement as early as their next earnings call that the retail giant would begin accepting Bitcoin. This was then picked up by Squawker.org who went on to note that Amazon Web Services had begun working with Digital Currency Group back in 2016.

With the rumor now swirling, it led to an article from Die Welt, a German national newspaper, that stated rumors were running in Silicon Valley that Amazon would soon accept the digital currency. This was further echoed by Cointelegraph today with another article on the topic.

You can already shop on Amazon using Bitcoin with Purse.io but having another company the size of Amazon supporting it natively would definitely give a strong use case for the revolutionary ledger. While on that thought, what an excellent acquisition Purse would actually make for the retail giant. Not only would you be picking up a company that is already saving your customers 20% over you own prices, you’d also get some of the brightest minds in the industry.

Source: https://squawker.org/technology/breaking-amazon-will-accept-bitcoin-by-october/

Coinbase Reverses Course on Bitcoin Naming

After issuing a blog entry on Monday stating that they would operate by naming the traditional bitcoin chain Bitcoin and refer to the forked chain as Bitcoin2x. Yesterday they then released a clarification stating they would be calling “the chain with the most accumulated difficulty Bitcoin.”

This immediately brought back the nervousness of the fork for many while others questioned the logic. If Coinbase was going to name the chain with the most most difficulty wouldn’t that mean that Bitcoin Cash should have shared the label of Bitcoin anytime it had surpassed traditional Bitcoin’s difficulty.

Will we see a new clarification issued reversing the reversal? I have to hope so, for Coinbase’s sake.

Source: https://blog.coinbase.com/clarification-on-the-upcoming-segwit2x-fork-d3c0f545c3e0
