Make A Mainnet Bitcoin Lightning Payment Today!

If you’ve never experienced the speed of a lightning transaction then you’ll enjoy taking this post for a spin. In it I’m going to show you how you can use Coinpanic’s Lightning Community Jar to send a mainnet bitcoin payment via the lightning protocol to complete a test transaction in the shopsteem.com store.

Note: This will not be sending any of your personal funds at all. This will be sending funds via Coinpanic’s Lightning Community Jar. Please be conscientious while using these resources.

1.) Open the ShopSteem.com Lightning Test Product and add it to the cart.

2.) View your cart by hovering over the cart icon or hitting the green highlight.

3.) Scroll down and click ‘Proceed to checkout ->’

4.) Enter fake information for your billing details (DO NOT ENTER REAL LIFE INFORMATION HERE!) then click the radial for Bitcoin followed by ‘Proceed to BTCPay’

5.) Select the Lightning Payment option in the upper right corner of the payment screen followed by ‘Copy’.

6.) Copy the payment address to your clipboard. (Hitting the lightning icon in the lower left will copy to your clipboard)

7.) Open a second window for Coinpanic’s Community Jar and paste your payment address on the right side under ‘Take from Jar’

8.) Make sure your have both windows open side by side before clicking ‘Pay Invoice’. You’ll want to see this!

9.) Hit ‘Pay Invoice’ but keep watching your order.

10.) You’ve just completed your Bitcoin lightning transaction. It wasn’t that hard at all now was it?


Depending on how well I stay connected to CoinPanic’s tool may mean that at times your transaction will fail. If so leave a comment and I’ll be happy to troubleshoot and let you know when you can give it another shot!

Hope you enjoyed it! Please do not attempt to test the Steem functionality at this time. It will not work! Let me repeat that part. Do NOT test the Steem payments part of the site. It will not work! I will let everyone know when you can test that part out and when I’ll start adding products to the site.

Today’s News

Nasdaq CEO says “Would Consider Creating Crypto Exchange”

Adena Friedman has expressed her thoughts that the Nasdaq would consider adding a crypto exchange to their services as the need arose. This could lead to a whole new level of regulated Bitcoin trading.

EOS Nears All Time High

EOS is approaching it’s all time highs again. Currently trading at $14.85, it is just below it’s all time high of $18.67 and may pass it soon.

Multiple Exchanges Reportedly Stopping ERC-20 Trading

It appears that multiple exchanges have stopped trading in ERC-20 tokens due to a potential smart contract bug. While trading will probably resume after services are ensured to be operating normally it reiterates that crypto platforms are still new and changing.

Bitstamp To Sell Exchange To Nexon?

It appears that Business Insider is reporting that Bitstamp, one of the oldest Bitcoin exchanges, may be selling itself to Counterstrike creator Nexon for $350 million. This would be huge news in my opinion but also a bad deal for the exchange. I would imagine their value as being much higher than just $350 million with Poloniex selling for $400 million recently.
