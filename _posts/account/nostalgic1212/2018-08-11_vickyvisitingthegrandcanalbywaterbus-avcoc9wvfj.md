In last few days, I've introduced the view of Gong Chen Bridge on the Grand Canal. Actually, I went there by water bus that day. This time, let me introduce to you the scenery along the water bus.

<a href="https://imgchr.com/i/P6RIj1"><img src="https://s1.ax1x.com/2018/08/11/P6RIj1.md.png" alt="P6RIj1.md.png" /><br/></a>

On the east side of Hangzhou Huancheng North Road and the Kunhe Center, you can see an 8-meter high sculpture  in the shape of anchor with the words "Wulin Men Quay".

This design of sculpture is based on the ship anchor of the Southern Song Dynasty. It looks like wood, but the core is made of copper which is simulated. The ribbon with the words "Wulin Men Quay" on the top of the sculpture not only represents the shape of Hangzhou section of the canal, but also combines canal culture and silk culture. It reproduces the prosperity of canal in the Southern Song Dynasty as well as the status of being the<strong>"Home of Silk"</strong>.

<a href="https://imgchr.com/i/P6RqAO"><img src="https://s1.ax1x.com/2018/08/11/P6RqAO.md.png" alt="P6RqAO.md.png" /><br/></a>

The stone archway is nearly 10 meters high with the beauty of  both ancient and modern. It is the entrance to the Wulin Men water bus of the Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal. It is composed of twelve upright stone pillars, which reflects the characteristics of the Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Cana from the aspect of geography as well as time. Four pairs of couplets written by famous artists are carved into stone pillars, and the top of the archway is engraved with the route map of Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal.

<a href="https://imgchr.com/i/P6RxgA"><img src="https://s1.ax1x.com/2018/08/11/P6RxgA.md.png" alt="P6RxgA.md.png" /><br/></a>

The building opposite the dock is one of Hangzhou's landmarks, the West Lake Cultural Square. We are ready to board the ship. The ticket price is three yuan, and you can use cash or Alipay.

<a href="https://imgchr.com/i/P6Wput"><img src="https://s1.ax1x.com/2018/08/11/P6Wput.md.jpg" alt="P6Wput.md.jpg" /><br/></a>

Qianlong Emperor (1711-1899) made southern tour 6 times through the Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal. They were 1751, 1757, 1762, 1765, 1780 and 1784.

In the 16th year of Qianlong (1751), the emperor Hong Li made a southern tour to check the payment of imperial grain in Jiangsu, Zhejiang and Anhui provinces. The province of Jiangsu and Anhui accumulated huge amounts of debt, but Zhejiang province did not default. The emperor was so pleased that exempted the land of Zhejiang province from the reward of 300,000 yuan. The local government engraved the <em>Imperial Decree</em> on the stone tablet in order to inform the officials and people.

<a href="https://imgchr.com/i/P6W9DP"><img src="https://s1.ax1x.com/2018/08/11/P6W9DP.md.png" alt="P6W9DP.md.png" /><br/></a>
