Around the middle of April, I was attracted by an article titled <strong><em>Did the Divine Director Step Down From the Altar</em></strong>. Since then, I learned about the movie <strong><em>The Third Murder</em></strong>, and the director mentioned in the article is <strong>Koreeda Hirokazu</strong>. To tell you the truth, I was not familiar with the name. In order to get some information about him, I turned over his data and it turns out that he had produced many famous films. For example: <strong><em>Air Doll</em></strong>, <strong><em>After the Storm</em></strong>, <strong><em>Kamakura Diary</em></strong> and <strong><em>Manbiki kazoku</em></strong>. These wonderful family films suddenly gives me the idea to have a good look at the movie of <strong><em>The Third Murder</em></strong>.

<a href="https://imgchr.com/i/PgM62R"><img src="https://s1.ax1x.com/2018/08/13/PgM62R.md.jpg" alt="PgM62R.md.jpg" /><br/></a>

The suspect <strong>Sankou (Koji Hashimoto)</strong> was charged with robbery and murder, and the defense attorney <strong>Shigeru (Fukuyama Masaharu)</strong> began an investigation. The confession of Sankou is different each time. From robbery to theft after murder to fiduciary murder. In the end, the suspect confessed that he had not killed anyone. The defense attorney became suspicious and began to suspect that Sankou was not the real criminal but that there was another case.

To conserve litigation resources, the judge allowed the prosecution to proceed in the same manner as before, even though he knew that the suspect might not the murder. The prosecutor or the lawyer cooperate with the judge, and they seems to reach a balance. In the eyes of this group: <strong>"The law is not the place to pursue the truth, but the place to adjust the stakes."</strong> I have seen this opinion in a Japanese TV drama called <a href="https://cnsteem.org/cn/@nostalgic1212/vicky-s-99-9">99.9% ï¼šåˆ‘äº‹å°‚é–€å¼è­·å£«</a>, which shows that it seems to be the common sense in the judicial system.

By this time, the appearance of the victim's daughter Saki (Suzu Hirose) changes the plot. The girl was willing to tell the secret of being raped by her father in order to exonerate the suspect. I think the reason Sankou wanted to take all the responsibility at the very first place is that he wanted to keep the girl from giving the embarrassing testimony. In this gloomy tale, the warmth between the suspect and the victim's daughter is unsentimental.

<img src="https://s1.ax1x.com/2018/08/13/PglvCQ.jpg" alt="PglvCQ.jpg" /><br/>

To some extent, I think the translator gave the audience a false impression and a wrong positioning for the film. <strong><em>The Third Murder</em></strong> is an ethical film about family and death under the cloak of law and suspense. What's more, the director did not clarify who was the killer, even the end of the film.
