<p>　　相信很多人都跟我一样，先通过学习唐代著名文学家、大才子王勃的《滕王阁赋》，然后才知道了滕王阁。</p>
<p><br>
　　到达南昌这所城市时，我选择了两处景点作为此行的这所城市的主要目的，一是众所周知的八一南昌起义陈列馆，另一个就是滕王阁景点了。</p>
<p><br>
　　滕王阁，江南三大名楼之一，位于南昌市东湖区，因为吸取了前两天在南京参观总统府时时间太晚(下午4:30)改为第二天的教训，我一大早吃过标准的南昌早餐(拌粉+煨汤)后，便向滕王阁那边出发，出发前我看了下，酒店离滕王阁位置不算很远，大约不到两公里，于是我便踩着共享单车出发了。</p>
<p>　　I believe many people like me, first learned by studying the Tang Dynasty famous writer, Wang Bo, the great genius of "The preface of Pavilion of Prince Teng", and then know the Pavilion of Prince Teng.</p>
<p>　　When I arrived in Nanchang, I chose two spots as the main purpose of this city. One is the well-known August 1 Nanchang Uprising Museum and the other is the Pavilion of Prince Teng.</p>
<p>　　the Pavilion of Prince Teng, one of the Three Great Towers in South of the Yangtze(sourthern China) is located in Donghu District, Nanchang City, because I had learned the lesson of the day before when I visited the Presidential Palace in Nanjing two days ago (4:30 pm) and changed to the next day, and I ate it early in the morning Standard Nanchang breakfast (mix powder + simmer soup), then set off to the side of Pavilion of Prince Teng I saw before departure, the hotel is located not far from the Pavilion of Prince Teng, about less than two kilometers, so I set foot on the shared bike departure.</p>

<p><br>
　　历史上的滕王阁，先后共重建达29次之多， 屡建屡毁，屡毁屡建，我看了下墙上的记载，最近一次是抗日战争期间被日军毁坏，民国期间本想修复，都已经找了著名建筑学家梁思成，但无奈各种原因终未完成，直到建国后最终由梁思成弟子主导下最终完成重建设计，当然，建筑主要是依据当年梁思成的图纸设计完成的，也就是现在大家看到的滕王阁面貌。</p>
<p><br>
　　滕王阁景区门票成人票50元，门口有人工售票，也有机器售票，支持银联卡、手机支付(微信和支付宝)等，非常方便。</p>
<p>　　In the history of the Pavilion of Prince Teng, it has been rebuilt a total of 29 times in succession. time and again destroyed and time and again rebuild. I read the records on the wall. The most recent one was destroyed by the Japanese during the Anti-Japanese War. The famous architect Liang Sicheng(the father of modern Chinese architecture) has been found. However, various reasons have not been fulfilled until the completion of the re-design by Liang Sicheng disciples eventually. After that, of course, the construction was mainly based on the drawings designed by Liang Sicheng, the Pavilion of Prince Teng face to see.</p>
