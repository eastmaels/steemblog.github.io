El ChaltÃ©n is a charming village located in the Los Glaciares National Park, near the border between Argentina and Chile. It is surrounded by magnificent peaks and stunning glacial lakes. This location makes El ChaltÃ©n a popular base for hiking trails. So it is named Argentina's Trekking Capital. I feel it's a bit more quiet and peaceful hiking in this region than in the more well-known Torres del Paine national park in Chile.

The main peak in the photos is the shining star - Cerro/Monte Fitz Roy, also known as Cerro ChaltÃ©n. "ChaltÃ©n" means smoking mountain in the local language of the nomadic hunters in Patagonia. Since the peak is covered by clouds most of the time, the natives believed it was a volcano. But when we were there, it's mostly sunny and pretty clear around the peak. :)
