Greetings everyone! Here's my shot of 2 monks at Phnom Bakheng near Siem Reap of Cambodia. It's my entry for @daveks' B&W Photo Contest themed **Colour Splash**. 

>Definition: The process of converting photos to monochrome, while keeping chosen details in colour.

##
When I saw this cool theme, the first images came to mind were the ones of monks in orange robe and dark grey temples. So I digged out this one I took in Angkor, and played with some color splash fun. :) I feel the bright-orange color of their robes forms a great contrast with the monochromatic towers and background. It's also interesting to catch the young dynamic monks and the old static towers in one frame. 

What about you? Have you tried applying "color splash" before? What kind of images do you think are suitable for this touch? 


##
Now a bit more info about the temple. =) Phnom Bakheng is located a bit north of Angkor Wat and south of Angkor Thom. Because it's on the highest hill in the area, it's been long attracting visitors for the sunset view over Angkor Wat. However, I was here during noon time, oops... so no sunset view but only a scorching fire ball right above my head... Hmmm, while the scene of these young monks in bright robe by the ancient temples is equally amazing, isn't it? 

Phnom Bakheng, the architectural treasure is the state temple of the first Khmer capital in the Angkor region. As one of the oldest temples in Angkor Archaeological Park, it is a temple mountain built at the end of the 9th century in honor of the Hindu god Shiva. 

It sits on a rectangular base and rises in five levels. After climbing some steep stairs (the stairs were not that many, but the heat and humidity in the jungle made it pretty hard), I took this photo at the top level where it's crowned by five main towers. These five sandstone sanctuaries stand in a quincunx pattern â€” one in the center and one at each corner of the levelâ€™s square. They were all in repair, and most of the original 108 small towers around the temple at different tiers had already collapsed.
