The initial railway system was built by the British in 1864, mainly to transport tea and coffee from the Tea Country to the capital port city Colombo, so that the produce can be shipped internationally. Today there are train tracks between major towns in Sri Lanka.

The coastal train ride from Colombo to Dutch colonial Galle is also popular among tourists and locals alike. In the following photo I took at the Marine Drive of Colombo, you can see the train rails. This running train was probably going to Galle or somewhere in between along the coast.
