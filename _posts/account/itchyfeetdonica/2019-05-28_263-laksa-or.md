##
In my picture above, it is a Laksa I had in 328 Katong Laksa on the East Coast Road of Singapore. In a 2013 cooking showdown, 328 Katong Laksa beat the celebrity chef Gordon Ramsay. This result surely made it even more famous. 

The Laksa was inspired by the Straits Chinese who live in the precinct of Katong. I quickly finished the aromatic seafood and rice vermicelli soaked in the spicy broth flavoured with coconut milk and dried shrimp. It tasted divine!

## 
Though we can find Laksa easily in Malaysia and Singapore, it's rare here in Belgium. There are only 3 to 4 Malaysian restaurants in the whole country (I haven't tried the 4th one yet as I can't find any pictures of it online but only a name and address, would check it out one day), and not every one of them sells Laksa. Luckily Belgium is small in size, so I'm grateful to enjoy some hearty Asian meals occasionally without driving too far. :) 

## 
![u514mf00sd.jpg](https://img.esteem.ws/u514mf00sd.jpg)

##
As for the precious Laksa here, it's normal that there's not a variety of choices like Nyonya Laksa, Johor Laksa, Sarawak Laksa, Penang Asam Laksa... but only the most common curry mee with coconut-based curry broth. I feel this is the more mild flavored Laksa comparing to other types I tried in Malaysia? Any Malaysian or Singaporean Steemians please correct me if I got it wrong. 

##
![nv26mg4ncm.jpg](https://img.esteem.ws/nv26mg4ncm.jpg)

##
As you can see from my photos, the toppings include prawns, fish cakes/balls, tofu puffs, bean sprouts, long beans, scallions, eggplants, and even char siu (Cantonese barbecue pork) pieces... It's rich and fragrant as always. Slurp, slurp, slurp~
