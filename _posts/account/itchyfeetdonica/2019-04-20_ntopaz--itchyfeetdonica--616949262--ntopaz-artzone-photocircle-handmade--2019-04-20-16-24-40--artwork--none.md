![0.makingaring.JPG](https://cdn.steemitimages.com/DQmbiiXoq89qL7Bm6G2o84bKpBDkEbcquUJ5NeGcT9F24U2/0.makingaring.JPG)

## 
**1**. 
Cut off a silver strip and flatten the rough edges with a hand file.

**2**. 
Put that precious piece of silver bar into acid bath for cleaning. 

**3**.  
Hammer the silver bar into a round(ish) shape - not an easy job, but so much fun to try this for the first time! :D

**4**.  
Solder the open silver round strip with another tiny piece of silver to close the loop. This brazing requires quite some skills and patience. I wouldn't have achieved it without the experienced master's help. 

**5**. 
Then it comes to the most time and energy consuming part! I had to hammer the silver loop hard over a mandrel to make it more round and get the nice facets. So the whole workshop room was filled with the beautiful hammering sounds~~noises~~ for a long time (not only from me, but also from other enthusiastic apprentices. ;D) After the hard work of hammering, my ring formed some unique metal facets. Then I used the sand paper (in the middle picture 5) to deburr the edges.  

**6**. 
At last, polish the ring with a polishing wheel. 


##
So that's it! My handmade, one of a kind silver ring is born!
