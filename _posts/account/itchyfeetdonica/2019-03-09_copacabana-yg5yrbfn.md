Hey, here are the photos I took with my phone in Copacabana, a Bolivian town close to the border with Peru. That late afternoon, I was wandering around in town by Lake Titicaca, the worldâ€™s highest navigable body of water. 

Suddenly I discovered a narrow trail leading uphill, so I decided to explore where it would lead to. For some parts I had to use my hands to climb up the steep cliffs, and it's not that easy to ascend in the very thin air.

It turned out to be totally worthy - when I reached the hill top at sunset, the view was just breathtaking (quite literally too as I almost lost my breath ;p). The city's colorful houses spreaded along the green hills, and the vast Lake Titicaca looked like an endless stretch of the deep blue sea. 

You might have heard of the world-renowned Copacabana beach in Rio de Janeiro? In fact, that more famous cousin takes its name from this town. :) In the local language Aymara, Copacabana means â€œview of the lakeâ€, deriving from â€œkota kawanaâ€. Later in Rio, people found the shape of their beach resembled the crescent one here in Bolivia (as you can see in my photos), so they called it the same name.
